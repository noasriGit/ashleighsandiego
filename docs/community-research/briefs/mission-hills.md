## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Recognized neighborhood within the Uptown Community Planning Area (City of San Diego); historic residential district west of Hillcrest core, bounded roughly by Old Town, Presidio Park, and Mission Hills canyons.
- **ZIP codes:** 92103 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** Hillcrest guide covers the commercial/walkable core (University Ave, medical district). Mission Hills is **quieter, architecturally distinct historic residential**—Presidio-adjacent hills, fewer bars/restaurants in-pocket, stronger historic-home buyer profile.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Early 20th-century single-family (Craftsman, Spanish Revival), some condos on canyon rims; limited new construction.
- Notable subpockets: Presidio Drive corridor; canyon-view streets above Washington St; pockets near Pioneer Park.
- Omissions / qualified language: Historic district overlays may apply; verify Mills Act eligibility per property, not neighborhood-wide.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks: Presidio Park, Pioneer Park, Montecito Park (nearby), canyon trail access.
- Commercial: Washington St and Goldfinch St village shops; Hillcrest dining 5–10 min walk downhill.
- Walkability / transit: Moderate walkability to Hillcrest/Old Town; MTS Route 10/105 serve Washington St corridor.
- Character: Residential, historic, quieter than Hillcrest; Old Town tourism adjacent but not in-core.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major freeways: I-5 (Old Town), I-8 (Mission Valley access via Washington).
- Typical destinations: Downtown (~10–15 min), airport SAN (~10 min), UCSD (~20–25 min), Sorrento Valley (~25 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Washington St and I-5 Old Town interchange congested at peaks.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified School District (verify by address).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
