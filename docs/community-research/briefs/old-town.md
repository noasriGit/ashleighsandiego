## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Historic district and surrounding residential blocks centered on Old Town San Diego State Historic Park, San Diego Ave, and Taylor St; within Old Town Community Plan area (City of San Diego).
- **ZIP codes:** 92110 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** Point Loma guide covers peninsula, Liberty Station, and coastal PL. Old Town is **historic-tourism core with adjacent residential** and trolley hub—not PL oceanfront or Liberty Station redevelopment focus.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Historic-adjacent condos and townhomes, small SFR pockets on hills above Old Town, some vacation rental near park.
- Notable subpockets: Harney St/Moore St residential; Presidio Hills; areas walking to Old Town Transit Center.
- Omissions / qualified language: Tourism foot traffic and event noise vary by block—buyers should visit weekday/weekend.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks/heritage: Old Town San Diego State Historic Park, Presidio Park (border), Heritage Park.
- Commercial: Old Town restaurants (Mexican heritage tourism), Friars Rd retail nearby.
- Walkability / transit: High; Old Town Transit Center (Green/Blue trolley, Amtrak, Coaster)—major regional hub.
- Character: Tourism-heavy daytime; transit-connected; historic identity; quieter on upper residential streets.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: I-5, I-8, San Diego Ave, Taylor St.
- Typical destinations: Downtown (~10 min trolley), airport (~10 min), UTC (~25 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Old Town trolley parking and event traffic; I-5/I-8 merge congestion.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (verify; Old Town addresses often feed Point Loma or Roosevelt cluster).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
