## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called coastal subarea at the base of La Jolla village centered on La Jolla Cove beach, Coast Blvd, and Prospect St; within La Jolla Community Planning Area.
- **ZIP codes:** 92037 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** La Jolla guide spans full peninsula market. Cove area is **tourist-dense coastal core** with sea lions, cliff dining, and limited parking—buyers seek walk-to-cove proximity vs mesa or Shores family beach lifestyle.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Luxury condos and townhomes on Coast Blvd/Prospect; historic cottages (limited turnover); very few detached SFR in immediate cove bowl.
- Notable subpockets: Coast Blvd oceanfront; upper Prospect St view units; Girard Ave walk streets.
- Omissions / qualified language: Short-term rental regulations affect some condos—verify HOA and city STR rules per unit.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks/beaches: La Jolla Cove, Ellen Browning Scripps Park, Coast Walk Trail.
- Commercial: Prospect St and Girard Ave dining; tourism-heavy retail.
- Walkability / transit: High walkability in village core; MTS Route 30/201 serve Torrey Pines Rd; parking scarce.
- Character: Tourist-adjacent coastal village; scenic; not quiet—sea lion colony and visitors define daily rhythm.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: Torrey Pines Rd, I-5 via Genesee or La Jolla Pkwy.
- Typical destinations: UCSD (~15 min), UTC (~12 min), downtown (~25 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Village traffic and event parking peak summer/weekends.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (La Jolla cluster—verify; many Cove condos feed Bird Rock/La Jolla elem boundaries).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
