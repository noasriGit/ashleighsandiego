## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called southern Pacific Beach neighborhood centered on Crown Point Drive, Mission Bay canals, and fan-shaped street grid east of Mission Blvd and north of Grand Ave approaches.
- **ZIP codes:** 92109 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** Pacific Beach guide covers Garnet Ave nightlife and beach boardwalk culture. Crown Point is **canal-side residential pocket** with family-oriented streets and Mission Bay access—not PB bar scene core.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: 1950s–1970s single-family on canal-adjacent lots, some duplexes; limited condos vs Mission Blvd corridor.
- Notable subpockets: Crown Point cul-de-sacs opening to Mission Bay; streets near Crown Point Park.
- Omissions / qualified language: Canal-front premiums vary; verify seawall/bulkhead maintenance responsibilities.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks: Crown Point Park, Mission Bay Park (Ski Beach, playground areas), Rose Creek trail.
- Commercial: Grand Ave and Mission Blvd (~1 mi); PB Garnet nightlife walkable for some blocks.
- Walkability / transit: Bike/walk to bay; MTS Route 8/9; beach ~15 min walk.
- Character: Residential, family, canal/kayak lifestyle; quieter than Garnet party strip.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: Mission Blvd, I-5, Grand Ave, I-8 via Mission Valley.
- Typical destinations: Downtown (~15 min), UTC (~20 min), airport (~15 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Mission Blvd summer congestion; Grand Ave event traffic.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (Bay Park/PB cluster—verify; Crown Point often feeds Bay Park schools).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
