## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called residential pocket within the Bay Park/Morena area of the Midway-Pacific Highway community planning context; generally the neighborhood west of Morena Blvd and east of Mission Bay, north of Friars Rd and south of Clairemont Dr.
- **ZIP codes:** 92110 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** Bay Park guide covers broader Morena Blvd hills and Bay Park core with canyon views. Bay Ho is **quieter Mission Bay-adjacent pocket** with smaller lots and strong bay-access identity—not the full Morena commercial redevelopment story.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: 1940s–1960s single-family on smaller lots, some duplexes; limited condo inventory vs Morena corridor.
- Notable subpockets: Streets walking distance to Mission Bay Park; areas near Bay Ho Community Park.
- Omissions / qualified language: "Bay Ho" naming is colloquial; some listings use "Bay Park" interchangeably—clarify pocket boundaries in FAQ.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks: Mission Bay Park (De Anza Cove, playgrounds), Bay Ho Community Park, Rose Canyon Open Space (nearby).
- Commercial: Morena Blvd emerging retail; Old Town and Liberty Station ~5–10 min drive.
- Walkability / transit: Moderate bay-side paths; MTS Route 9/27 on Morena; car helpful for groceries.
- Character: Quiet residential near bay recreation; family-oriented; less nightlife than PB/Mission Beach.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major freeways: I-5, I-8, SR-163 via Morena.
- Typical destinations: Downtown (~12 min), airport (~10 min), UTC (~18 min), beach (~10 min to Mission Beach)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Morena Blvd construction/redevelopment may affect commute seasonally.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (Bay Park / Clairemont cluster—verify by address).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
