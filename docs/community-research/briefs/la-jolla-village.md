## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called commercial and residential core of La Jolla centered on Girard Ave, Prospect St, Wall St, and La Jolla Parkway approaches—distinct from outlying mesa and beach subareas.
- **ZIP codes:** 92037 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** La Jolla guide covers full peninsula. Village is **walkable retail/dining hub** with mixed condos and upper village hills—not Shores beach family pocket or Torrey Pines mesa.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Condos and townhomes near Girard/Prospect; hillside SFR above village; mixed-age inventory (1960s condos to newer infill).
- Notable subpockets: Upper Girard; Fay Ave corridor; La Jolla Financial Building area condos.
- Omissions / qualified language: "Village" boundaries informal—some listings use "La Jolla" without subarea tag.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Commercial: Girard Ave, Prospect St, Wall St shops and restaurants; La Jolla Playhouse, Museum of Contemporary Art San Diego (La Jolla).
- Parks: Scripps Park (Cove edge), Ellen Browning Scripps Park, village pocket parks.
- Walkability / transit: Highest walkability in La Jolla; MTS 30/201 on Torrey Pines Rd.
- Character: Upscale village retail; walk-to-dining; blends local residents and visitors.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: Torrey Pines Rd, La Jolla Pkwy, I-5.
- Typical destinations: UCSD (~12 min), UTC (~10 min), downtown (~25 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Village parking and summer tourism affect daily errands.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (verify; village addresses often Bird Rock or La Jolla elem zones).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
