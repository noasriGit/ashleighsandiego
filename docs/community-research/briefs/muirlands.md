## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called hillside residential enclave in central La Jolla on Mount Soledad slopes, generally above La Jolla Blvd and west of Muirlands Drive, characterized by canyon and ocean views.
- **ZIP codes:** 92037 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** La Jolla guide spans peninsula. Muirlands is **view-driven hillside SFR enclave** with winding streets and limited commercial—distinct from flat Shores, tourist Cove, or Bird Rock Blvd retail.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Custom and semi-custom single-family (mid-century to contemporary), large lots on slopes; very limited multi-family.
- Notable subpockets: Muirlands Drive ridge lines; canyons bordering Soledad slopes; upper view streets.
- Omissions / qualified language: Steep driveways and geologic/soils reports common—buyers should verify slope stability disclosures.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks: Mount Soledad Natural Park adjacency, canyon trail access, short drive to La Jolla beaches.
- Commercial: Bird Rock/La Jolla Blvd (~5 min); village (~10 min).
- Walkability / transit: Low walkability (hills); car essential; MTS on La Jolla Blvd at base.
- Character: Quiet, view-oriented residential; privacy-focused; not walk-to-beach flat.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: Muirlands Dr, La Jolla Blvd, Torrey Pines Rd, I-5.
- Typical destinations: UCSD (~12 min), UTC (~12 min), downtown (~22 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Hillside streets narrow; school traffic on Muirlands Dr mornings.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (Bird Rock/La Jolla school zones—verify by address).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
