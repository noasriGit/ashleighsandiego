## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called elevated La Jolla neighborhoods surrounding Mount Soledad peak and memorial, including slopes above Pacific Beach border and east La Jolla mesa streets with panoramic views.
- **ZIP codes:** 92037 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** La Jolla guide covers peninsula broadly. Mount Soledad area emphasizes **elevation, 360° views, and memorial park landmark**—not beach-flat Shores or commercial village core.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: View-oriented single-family on slopes (1960s–2000s), some contemporary rebuilds; limited condos on lower slopes.
- Notable subpockets: Streets directly facing memorial; east slope toward I-5; border streets near Pacific Beach.
- Omissions / qualified language: View corridors protected inconsistently; verify CC&Rs and future construction sight-line impacts per property.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks: Mount Soledad Veterans Memorial and natural park, hiking paths, sunset viewpoints.
- Commercial: Bird Rock/La Jolla Blvd and PB Garnet Ave (~10 min drive).
- Walkability / transit: Low; car primary; memorial attracts visitors weekends.
- Character: View-driven residential; landmark tourism at peak; quieter residential side streets off memorial loop.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: Nautilus St, La Jolla Blvd, I-5, I-805 via mesa connectors.
- Typical destinations: UCSD (~15 min), UTC (~15 min), downtown (~20 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: Memorial weekend parking/traffic; steep streets in rain.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (PB/La Jolla cluster boundaries split by street—verify carefully).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
