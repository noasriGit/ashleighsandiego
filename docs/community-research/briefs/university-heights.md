## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Recognized Uptown neighborhood between Hillcrest and North Park, centered on Park Blvd and University Ave, including Vermont Street canyon pocket and Adams Ave fringe within Uptown Community Plan.
- **ZIP codes:** 92116 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** Hillcrest guide covers medical district and University Ave west of Park. University Heights is **Park Blvd-corridor residential** with its own Adams Ave node and Vermont St canyon—distinct ZIP (92116) and quieter residential scale than Hillcrest core.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Craftsman and bungalow single-family, small apartment buildings, some condos; mix of owner and renter-occupied.
- Notable subpockets: Vermont Street canyon rim; Park Blvd commercial edge; Adams Ave dining strip (border with Normal Heights).
- Omissions / qualified language: 92116 vs 92103 Hillcrest ZIP boundary confuses buyers—document clearly.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Commercial: Park Blvd local shops; Adams Ave restaurants (Tribute Pizza corridor); proximity to Hillcrest dining downhill.
- Parks: Vermont Street Canyon Open Space, Trolley Barn Park (nearby), Balboa Park edge (~1 mi).
- Walkability / transit: Moderate-high; MTS Route 2/11 on Park/University; Hillcrest walkable.
- Character: Walkable urban residential; LGBTQ+-friendly Uptown context; less hospital/medical traffic than Hillcrest.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major routes: Park Blvd, University Ave, I-805, SR-163 via Washington.
- Typical destinations: Downtown (~10 min), UCSD Medical Hillcrest (in-adjacent), airport (~12 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: University Ave traffic; Adams Ave weekend dining parking.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (verify; often Birney or Roosevelt cluster).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
