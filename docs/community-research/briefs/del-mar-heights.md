## 1. Geography & boundaries

- **Official definition:** Commonly called residential hill neighborhood east of I-5 and north Del Mar, within San Diego city limits (not incorporated Del Mar). Boundaries roughly follow Carmel Valley Rd south, El Camino Real east, and highland ridges above Del Mar mesa.
- **ZIP codes:** 92014 (cross-check `src/data/community-zips.ts`)
- **Relationship to parent:** Del Mar guide covers coastal village, fairgrounds, and beach town character. Del Mar Heights is **inland hillside residential** with canyon views, SD Unified schools, and I-5 commute—no beach walkability or Del Mar village dining in-pocket.

## 2. Housing stock

- Dominant types: Single-family detached (1970s–1990s tracts), some view custom homes; limited condos vs Carmel Valley.
- Notable subpockets: Streets with Los Peñasquitos Canyon views; pockets near Del Mar Heights Rd commercial strip.
- Omissions / qualified language: Shares 92014 ZIP with Del Mar city—clarify municipal boundary in buyer FAQ.

## 3. Lifestyle & anchors

- Parks: Los Peñasquitos Canyon Preserve access, Ashley Falls Park, nearby Carmel Valley parks.
- Commercial: Del Mar Heights Rd (Vons, local services); Del Mar village and UTC ~10–15 min drive.
- Walkability / transit: Car-oriented; MTS routes on Carmel Valley Rd / Del Mar Heights Rd.
- Character: Suburban family residential; quieter than coastal Del Mar; canyon trails vs beach lifestyle.

## 4. Commute & connectivity

- Major freeways: I-5, SR-56, I-805 via Carmel Valley.
- Typical destinations: Sorrento Valley (~10–15 min), UTC (~10 min), downtown (~25 min), Del Mar beach (~10 min)—roughly, depends on time of day.
- Qualifiers: I-5 Carmel Valley merge is peak choke point.

## 5. Schools

- School districts: San Diego Unified (Torrey Pines HS cluster for many addresses—verify).
- FAQ pattern: "Research specific school boundaries before buying"
- **Do NOT** claim rankings without official source + date
